Szell Handcuffs Rustlers, Mavericks Seize 1-0 Advantage in GCS
The stakes were high. Scott Szell was ready.

The right-hander turned in a performance for the ages at Rustlers Grounds today, pitching a 9-inning, complete-game shutout to lead the Houston Mavericks to victory in Game 1 of the Grand Championship Series. Szell held the Dallas Rustlers to 5 hits and 1 walk, while striking out 9. The win got the best-of-7 series off to a good start for Houston, which now leads 1-0.

Szell, who was born in Temescal Valley, California, threw 120 pitches over 9 innings, while allowing no runs.

"I had a good game plan," said Szell. "I made my pitches." His manager concurred. "It was all there," Carlos Sanchez said, "the velocity, the sharpness of his pitches."

The next game is set to be played tomorrow at Rustlers Grounds in Dallas.
