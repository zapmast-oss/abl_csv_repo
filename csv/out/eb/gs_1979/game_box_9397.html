Mavericks KO Firebirds
Sometimes the winner is the one who got the lucky bounces and the lucky calls, and sometimes the winner is just the one who played better.

It was the latter case in 1979, as the Houston Mavericks topped the Phoenix Firebirds 5-3 tonight at Phoenix Grounds to win the Grand Championship Series. It was the 1st title in franchise history.

"When you play for championships, you have to play better than the team in the other dugout. We did that in this series, and all year. Now we're the best team in baseball," said Houston shortstop Felix Alvarez, who was named series MVP.

The Mavericks took the series 4-1. The new titleholders completed the regular season with a first-place finish in the American Baseball Conference Central Division and a 96-66 record.

Houston manager Carlos Sanchez grinned and nodded when asked to comment on Alvarez's words.

"Felix said it best -- we played better than the other team," the skipper said.

Hometown officials announced they were already planning a "grand celebration" for the winners in the days to come.
