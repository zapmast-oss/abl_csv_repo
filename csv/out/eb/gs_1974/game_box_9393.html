Express Even With Hurricanes After Extra-Innings Win
The Pittsburgh Express nearly wasted a strong game from starter Antonio Hernandez, but were saved by some timely hitting in a walk-off win over the Miami Hurricanes today.

Hernandez threw 7.0 innings allowing 2 runs. The win allowed Pittsburgh to force a 2-2 tie in the best-of-7 Grand Championship Series.

The clutch at-bat came in the bottom of the 11th inning, when Pittsburgh designated hitter Antonio Serrano, facing Josh Maras, had a run-scoring sacrifice fly. It gave the Express the 3-2 win.

"There are no moral victories," said losing manager Seth Coe.

The next game is set to be played tomorrow at Express Stadium in Pittsburgh.
