Hurricanes Triumph in 1974 Grand Championship Series
The Miami Hurricanes ignited a huge celebration as they beat the Pittsburgh Express 7-2 to win the Grand Championship Series.

Miami won the series 4-3, capturing the title for the 1st time.

"We stepped it up at the right time, and now we're the champs," said Miami manager Seth Coe. "It's a great feeling."

The champs finished the regular season 91-71 and claimed first place in the National Baseball Conference Eastern Division.

In the jubilant clubhouse after the decisive game, series MVP and third baseman Cory Lynn said the Hurricanes have "great fans and they deserve this."

"I am so happy for our city right now," he said.
