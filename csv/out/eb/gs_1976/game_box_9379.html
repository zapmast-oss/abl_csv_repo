Ellsworth Blasts Kings, Aces Take Game 1, 5-3
The New York Aces started the Grand Championship Series in style today, winning on the road against the Atlanta Kings at Kings Field by a score of 5-3 to take an early 1-0 lead in the best-of-7 series. Aces right fielder Logan Ellsworth excelled. He was 3-4 with a home run and a double. He drove in 2 runs and scored once.

The game turned with no one out in the top of the fourth when Ellsworth, who finished 3rd in the ABC in extra base hits with 63 in 1976, hit a 2-run home run. It propelled the Aces into the lead, 3-1.

"It was a good day but I'm not letting my guard down," Ellsworth said. "I'll let my guard down as soon as someone puts a big, fat championship ring on my finger."

The next game is set to be played tomorrow at Kings Field in Atlanta.
